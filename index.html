Here is the brief introduction of the on-going project:

Title:Camera Parameters are Changing: Analysis of Nonlinear Systems and Auto-Calibration with License Plates.
Camera auto-calibration or self-calibration can save a lot of human effort for calibrating with a chessboard. In real-time scenarios like autonomous driving, self-calibration makes it possible to dynamically correct camera parameters, increasing the accuracy of image information, thus enforces safety.
One major issue of auto-calibration is to match image points captured by the camera to physical points with known 3D positions in the real world. Previous works involve picking points from cars, walking humans, road lines, and traffic signs. However, they either require specific geometry, or have trouble with shape or position changes, which prevent them from being generalizable. Another problem to be solved is, how many feature points, under a certain amount of noise, are enough for a good calibration. People believe that the more points we have, the better the calibration will be. Yet in many cases, the number of available feature points is limited (by the algorithms or the environment). Can we estimate the quality of calibration based on the number of feature points we have? Mathematical models are needed to analyze these nonlinear systems.
